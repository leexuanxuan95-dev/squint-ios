Privacy Policy

Last updated: 7 June 2026

Squint is built so there is nothing to collect. We do not operate a server, and the app contains no analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking SDKs.

What we collect

Nothing leaves your device. Squint's App Privacy label is "Data Not Collected" in every category.

Camera & face data

The front TrueDepth camera is used solely to compute the distance between your face and the screen so test letters are sized correctly. Squint reads only a single distance value. The camera image, depth map, and face mesh are never stored and never transmitted.

Where your data lives

Your self-check history, eye-break records, and journal entries are stored locally on your device under Apple's file protection. If you subscribe to Plus and enable iCloud sync, this data is synced through your own private, end-to-end-encrypted iCloud container. We cannot access it.

Health data

If you allow it, Squint writes a 20-second "mindful session" to Apple Health each time you complete a 20-20-20 break. This stays in your Health app on your device. Squint does not read or upload your other health data.

On-device intelligence

The optional weekly Ergonomics Plan (Plus) runs entirely on-device using Apple's on-device language model. No prompt or output is sent to any server.

Children

Squint does not register users. Family members under 13 are added only through Apple Family Sharing by a family organizer.
